Why this interactive prototype?

Each year the course of Human-Computer Interaction (HCi) at EPFL taught by Dr. Pearl Pu provides to the students a Design Challange to tackle. The idea is to simulate a full design thinking process from conception, interviews, prototyping and finally evaluation of the results.
This year's challange is called "AI is beautiful" and aims to develop a system thanks information visualization techniques that could explain AI, thus making the inner logic of AI more accessible and scrutable to a lay user.

The challenge requires the final solution to stay within the following three criteria:

  1. Big data - the final solution must be able to treat a large amount of data
  2. Data transformation – the AI algorithm and techniques chosen are capable
    of transforming data into more meaningful insights
  3. Relatedness – lay users can easily relate to the final solution, in its interactive and visual form
