The dataset behind the information visualisation

In order to develop an InfoViz that people could relate to, a "good" dataset needed to be selected. In this scope, a "good" dataset should have enough data to be parsed by an AI system (more than 500 samples for example) and consist of real data that each of us could easily understand.

The project was conducted on the famous GTZAN dataset adopted as well for the paper on Music Genre Recognition "Musical genre classification of audio signals" by G. Tzanetakis and P. Cook (2002).
The dataset consists of music samples, collected in 2000-2001 from a variety of sources including personal CDs, radio, microphone recordings, in order to represent a variety of recording conditions. The dataset is not labeled with titles, artists nor any other metadata on each recording, but thanks to some third-party help the majority of such missing information has been crowdsurcely collected which made the InfoViz possible.

Musical genres

The dataset consists of 1000 audio tracks each 30 seconds long. It contains 10 genres, each represented by 100 tracks. The tracks are all 22050Hz Mono 16-bit audio files.
